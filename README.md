# Women's Football Mental Wellness Initiative

> A research-backed resource on mental wellness and anxiety interventions for female athletes, drawing on clinical trial evidence from ClinicalTrials.gov.

---

## About

This repository supports a **mental wellness initiative for elite women's football teams**. It aggregates findings from registered clinical trials on psychological and physiological interventions relevant to performance anxiety, mindfulness training, emotion regulation, burnout prevention, and the nutritional underpinnings of mental health.

---

## Why Mental Wellness Matters in Women's Football

| Challenge | Impact |
|---|---|
| Performance anxiety | Reduced execution, attentional narrowing, injury risk |
| Season-long stress | Burnout, fatigue, reduced motivation |
| Female Athlete Triad / RED-S | Hormonal disruption, bone loss, anxiety, depression |
| Under-resourced mental health support | Stigma, unmet need |

## Key Findings from ClinicalTrials.gov

**Search query:** "anxiety intervention female athletes" — **16 studies identified**

---

### Study 1: WBV & Relaxation for Adolescent Female Athletes with Triad Syndrome
**NCT07587346** | MTI University, Egypt | Recruiting | 8 weeks | n=50 (ages 14-16)

**Intervention:** Whole-Body Vibration (WBV) + relaxation techniques (Progressive Muscle Relaxation, mindfulness meditation, diaphragmatic breathing, autogenic training), 3x/week.

**Key outcomes:**
- Primary: Bone mineral density (DEXA) and hormonal regulation (estradiol)
- Secondary: **Anxiety** via Hamilton Anxiety Rating Scale (HAM-A) — 14-item, 5-point Likert

**Implication for football:** PMR and mindfulness are being clinically validated for anxiety reduction in young female athletes. Relevant for U16/U18 academy players experiencing training load stress, menstrual irregularities, or disordered eating risk.

---

### Study 2: Stroboscopic Training & Neuro-Psychophysiological Responses in Female Athletes
**NCT07392385** | Bozok University, Turkey | Completed (2025) | 8 weeks | n=42 (ages 10-16)

**Intervention:** Sport-specific stroboscopic training (glasses intermittently restricting visual input) vs. conventional training in novice, intermediate, and elite female volleyball players.

**Key outcomes:**
- Primary: Prefrontal cortical activation (fNIRS), Heart Rate Variability (HRV), dual-task performance, Electrodermal Activity (EDA), eye-tracking
- Secondary: **Depression, anxiety, and stress** via validated self-report questionnaire

**Implication for football:** Perceptual-cognitive training simultaneously improves anxiety-related physiological markers (HRV, EDA) and on-pitch performance — a dual-benefit model directly transferable to football-specific decision-making drills.

---

### Study 3: Prevention of Energy Deficit Syndrome (RED-S) in Female Athletes
**NCT05567211** | University of Alicante, Spain | Completed (2022-2023) | 12 weeks | n=50

**Intervention:** Single-blind RCT with personalised nutrition (Mediterranean diet or red berry antioxidant protocol) + exercise + app-based monitoring to prevent RED-S.

**Key outcomes:**
- **Mood state / tension-anxiety:** Profile of Mood States (POMS)
- Quality of life: SF-36
- Eating behaviour risk: EAT-26
- Menstrual health: RED-S CAT questionnaire

**Implication for football:** 87% of female athletes show at least one RED-S symptom, and nutritional deficits directly drive anxiety and mood disturbance. A multidisciplinary approach with digital monitoring is the evidence-based standard for women's football squads.

## How to Use This Resource

- **Sport Psychologists:** HAM-A and POMS are recommended baseline screening tools for squads.
- **Coaches & Performance Staff:** Stroboscopic training formats improve both HRV and decision-making — consider embedding in football drills.
- **Medical & Welfare Teams:** Run RED-S screening (LEAF-Q + EAT-26) as routine pre-season assessment — 87% prevalence makes this standard care.
- **Academy Staff:** The WBV + relaxation protocol (NCT07587346) is designed for ages 14-16 and addresses physical and mental health together.
- **Players:** Mindfulness micro-dosing (15-20 min, 3x/week) offers stigma-free, flexible mental skills development.
